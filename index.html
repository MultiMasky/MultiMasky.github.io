Unlock: Leadership


A 3D online, multiplayer serious game - Unlock: Leadership provides an environment for learners to practice their leadership skills and think about the way they interact with others. The game is set on a tropical island with the backdrop of a race against time to stop an apocalyptic event, with a number of fiendish puzzles requiring teamwork and cooperation barring your way.

On this project I served as an assistant in minor aspects of design, providing critique and managing quality assurance and testing sessions. I would regularly provide feedback on usability and user experience. Later I would also source audio for the game as part of sound design.

Reception to the game has thus far been overwhelmingly positive at events and showcases. It has recently been released and being negotiated for sale to various organizations and training providers.

  • Client: Totem Learning
  • Date: September 2015
  • Service: Audio, Design & Testing

Green IT


(Note: Please excuse the whitespace border in the video. The game was not created for this resolution!)

This is an educational game seeking to teach management and IT professionals in smaller companies about ways their operations could be made more cost-effective and environmentally friendly, whether they are a large data centre or an IT office. It is largely a hidden object for those that may not necessarily have a lot of experience with games.

I led the project management, design, creative direction and localization for this product. As is my style, I worked very closely with the artists and developers involved at each stage and constantly iterated on the design from feedback I sought from the partners.

The game was released as part of the Is IT Green project comprised of numerous international partners and deemed very successful at achieving the learning outcomes in focus tests and subsequent use in training days. It has been translated into 8 languages.

  • Client: Is IT Green
  • Date: August 2015
  • Service: Design, Management & Writing

Last Aegis


Please note that this project is still a work-in-progress, with many placeholder assets and systems being tested or implemented gradually. It still represents a body of work that I am proud of: a tactical roleplaying game in space with anime influences and a character-driven narrative.

This prototype is being worked on by a team of 7 that I assembled and now manage across development sprints. In addition, I am the primary driving force behind the creative vision, systems design and narrative. It is a demanding task but extremely rewarding - I try to inspire by conveying the spirit of the game through involvement and discussions with all disciplines, rather than leading through micromanagement. The results and wealth of experience I've received have been fantastic.

My design largely focuses around combat systems and balancing. I am looking to create a strategy game with a great deal of important decisions to make at each stage, with considerations such as component targeting and firing arcs for depth. Customisation of ships to accommodate differing playstyles is also an integral feature important both for the player experience and the tactical variety available.

  • Client: Periphery Games
  • Date: Ongoing
  • Service: Design, Development & Management

Design Quest


Approached by a private client, I was asked to create a small proof-of-concept game to be used in gathering research for a thesis. The concept revolved around being an intern at a design studio that had to accomplish various tasks much like a roleplaying game. The content and much of the flow of the game was to be provided, but as this individual had little game design expertise, it fell to me to interpret their vision into a workable prototype.

I served as the primary point of contact and deliberation between my team and the client, looking to strike a balance between expectations and reality. Much of my work entailed deciphering and presenting the materials in a suitable format for the development team, making design adjustments where necessary in consultation with the client, arranging the voice acting and creating some of the UI elements.

The project was successfully delivered to the client and was used as part of the thesis as planned. In addition to the third-person perspective and some roleplaying game mechanics, we integrated cutscenes with voice-acting into the experience as requested by the client.

  • Client: Periphery Games
  • Date: March 2015
  • Service: Design, Development & Management

Dream Job


Remploy is a leading organization in helping those with disabilities find employment. I was contracted by my employer to assist an external studio in the design of a game that would serve as a cursory introduction to the process of seeking a job for Remploy's clientele as part of their digital skills training.

This was a challenging exercise as I was replacing another designer, working with an external team, a tiny budget and had no previous experience in design for disability consideration.

I made it a focus to align Remploy's understanding and expectations with that of the project's budget, which my predecessor had been unable to do. I also tapped into the feedback of Remploy's focus testing and subject matter specialists to create a superhero theme that really resonated with their client experience.

Remploy were very happy with the final product and incorporated it into their digital skills training on their website where it is playable by visitors.

  • Client: Remploy
  • Date: July 2014
  • Service: Design

Kindred


This demonstration was conceived as part of a university module on level design. The player would need to switch between two characters that exhibited contrasting attributes to solve puzzles cooperatively. One could move fast and jump, the other was sluggish and would only float, but was able to manipulate objects in the environment from a top-down view.

The music and visual assets were all sourced free, but received modifications prior to implementation. Each path taken by the respective characters was designed to showcase their unique characteristics. Discovery of some aspects were intended to occur naturally, such as with the second character's floaty descent incidentally being brought to the player's attention after the first ramp, to be immediately applied to the puzzle thereafter.

Along with an accompanying write-up this project received a mark in the First-class honours percentile.

  • Client: Coventry University
  • Date: April 2012
  • Service: Design & Development

Split

The central mechanic of this platformer was the ability to switch between two dimensions which embodied the opposing cultural influences of individualism and collectivism. The levels would necessitate switching to navigate environmental pathfinding puzzles, but would also be integral to problem-solving an impasse with characters in the course of the main narrative.

My role was that of a level designer and developer. I worked alongside other developers and 3D modellers in a closely-knit team, interpreting the vision of the creative lead and coming up with mechanics and level layouts that showcased them. For this I drew on the inspiration of Super Mario design: teaching single mechanics to a player in a safe space, incorporating it into the subsequent level, before challenging the player to demonstrate their acquired skill with a flourish by the end.

Although built primarily as a contribution to academic research, Split was later released on the Google Play store and has received positive reviews.

  • Client: Serious Games Institute
  • Date: April 2013
  • Service: Design & Development

NWN: World Building

Neverwinter Nights was a multiplayer roleplaying game based on Dungeons & Dragons. In addition to the core game there were also extensive content creation tools, enabling an enthusiastic community of modders to spring up around online persistent worlds.

Aside from playing and acting as a game master for numerous different servers, my aptitude with the toolset for world building and level design was well-known in several circles. I was approached with an offer to create a large amount of content for a server known as Sigil: Planar Legends that later achieved a playerbase of 40+ players at peak times during my administration.

In addition to the challenges of integrating my work with pre-existing content, I needed to work from source material based on the setting of Planescape. I faithfully recreated the realms, but did so while balancing considerations such as server resource and user experience, making areas that became iconic and the server-centre for the playerbase.

  • Client: Sigil: Planar Legends
  • Date: July 2011
  • Service: Design, Development & Management
